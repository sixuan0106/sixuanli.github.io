Research

Large Language Models and Investment Advisory

Working Paper

This paper investigates how integrating large language models, such as ChatGPT or DeepSeek, into robo-advisory platforms can improve investor decision-making. Using financial literacy as the main setting, the study examines investor engagement, understanding, ETF adoption, international diversification, and portfolio outcomes.

Educating Entrepreneurs on VC Contracts

Working Paper

This paper conducts a randomized controlled trial to examine how contract education and legal consulting affect entrepreneurs' outcomes in venture capital and seed funding. The results suggest that contract knowledge helps entrepreneurs make more informed career choices and negotiate better terms, while also reducing entry into the VC funding pipeline.

Sanctions and Social Capital: Evidence from the Russian Invasion of Ukraine

Working Paper

This paper studies what determines firms' decisions to exit Russia after the Ukraine invasion and whether investors value such social image management. The evidence links exit decisions to ESG, country-level security concerns, public awareness, institutions, legal origin, and sanctions, but finds no stock-market reputation premium for exiting firms.

Large Language Models and Portfolio Construction

Working Paper

This paper examines whether large language models can use reasoning capabilities to generate portfolio recommendations beyond conventional textual analysis. The study uses Wall Street Journal articles from the U.S. and Chinese government policy announcements to evaluate ChatGPT-generated portfolios.

Medical Boards and CEOs

Publication

This study examines the role of medical expertise among board members and senior executives. Using the COVID-19 outbreak in China as a natural experiment, it finds that firms with medical expertise, excluding healthcare and pharmaceutical firms, experience significantly higher stock returns than firms without such expertise.
