Working Papers

Fostering Social-Media Entrepreneurs: Evidence from A Field Experiment in China
Fangzhou Lu, Sixuan Li, and Yingyan Shi
[Paper]
Abstract

This paper conducts a field experiment in China, offering startups and SMEs conditional social media subsidies to stimulate business growth. The intervention increases revenue, employment, and access to fintech credit, especially for firms with high digital potential.

Robo-Advising Meets Large Language Models: Educating Investors on Alpha and Beta of Mutual Funds and Stocks
Fangzhou Lu, Sixuan Li, and Yingyan Shi
[Paper] [DOI]
Abstract

This paper investigates how integrating large language models into robo-advisory platforms can improve investor decision-making. In a pilot program with a major brokerage firm, the LLM-enabled robo-advisor provides personalized, conversational support to investors and improves investor engagement, understanding, ETF adoption, diversification, and returns.

Educating Entrepreneurs on VC Contracts
Fangzhou Lu, Sixuan Li, and Yingyan Shi
[Paper]
Abstract

This paper conducts a randomized controlled trial to examine how contract education and contract legal consulting affect entrepreneurs' outcomes in venture capital and seed funding. The findings suggest that greater contract knowledge helps entrepreneurs make more informed career choices and negotiate better terms, while also reducing entry into the VC funding pipeline.

Sanctions and Social Capital: Evidence from the Russian Invasion of Ukraine
Fangzhou Lu, Lei Huang, and Sixuan Li
[Paper] [DOI]
Abstract

This paper examines what determines firms' decisions to exit Russia after the Ukraine invasion and whether investors value such social image management. The evidence links exit decisions to ESG scores and country-level characteristics, but finds no stock market reputation premium relative to firms that remain.

ChatGPT, Generative AI, and Investment Advisory
Fangzhou Lu, Lei Huang, and Sixuan Li
[Paper] [DOI]
Abstract

This paper examines whether large language models can use reasoning capabilities to generate portfolio recommendations beyond conventional textual analysis. Using Wall Street Journal articles and Chinese government policy announcements, the study evaluates ChatGPT-generated portfolios in out-of-sample tests.

Publications

Medical Boards and CEOs
Dunhong Jin, Fangzhou Lu, Sixuan Li, and Chao Yan
Review of Corporate Finance Studies, forthcoming.
[Paper] [DOI] [RCFS Forthcoming]
Abstract

This study examines the role of medical expertise among board members and senior executives. Using the COVID-19 outbreak in China as a natural experiment, it finds that firms with medical expertise, excluding healthcare and pharmaceutical firms, experience significantly higher stock returns than firms without such expertise.
