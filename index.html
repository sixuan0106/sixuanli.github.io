About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at The University of Hong Kong. My research interests include FinTech, large language models, and corporate finance.

Working Papers

Fostering Social-Media Entrepreneurs: Evidence from A Field Experiment in China

with Fangzhou Lu and Yingyan Shi

This paper studies a field experiment in China that offered startups and SMEs conditional social-media subsidies to stimulate business growth. The evidence points to a fintech accelerator mechanism: digital investment increases sales, digital footprints, customer ratings, and access to fintech credit.

Presentations: 2025 NBER Place-Based Policies and Entrepreneurship conference; 2025 PacDev conference jointly hosted by UCLA and USC; NYU CGEB seminar; HKU seminar.

Robo-Advising Meets LLM: Educating Investors on Alpha and Beta of Mutual Funds and Stocks

with Fangzhou Lu and Yingyan Shi

This paper investigates how integrating LLMs into robo-advisory platforms can improve investor decision-making in a financial-literacy setting. The LLM-augmented robo-advisor increases investor engagement and understanding, encourages ETF adoption, and generates spillovers to international diversification.

Educating Entrepreneurs on VC Contracts

with Fangzhou Lu and Yingyan Shi

This randomized controlled trial examines how contract education and legal consulting affect entrepreneurs' outcomes in venture capital and seed funding. Greater contract knowledge helps entrepreneurs make more informed career choices and negotiate better terms, while reducing entry into the VC funding pipeline.

SSRN

Sanctions and Social Capital: Evidence from the Russian Invasion of Ukraine

with Fangzhou Lu and Lei Huang

This paper examines firms' decisions to exit Russia after the Ukraine invasion and whether investors value social image management. The findings support a reputation-concern channel rather than purely altruistic motives.

SSRN

ChatGPT, Generative AI, and Investment Advisory

with Fangzhou Lu and Lei Huang

This paper examines whether large language models can use reasoning capabilities to generate portfolio recommendations beyond conventional textual analysis, using Wall Street Journal articles and Chinese government policy announcements.

Medical Boards and CEOs

with Dunhong Jin, Fangzhou Lu, and Chao Yan

This study uses the COVID-19 outbreak in China as a natural experiment and examines whether firms with medical expertise among directors and senior executives perform differently from firms without such expertise.
